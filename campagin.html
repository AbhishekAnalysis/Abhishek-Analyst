Project Overview

What & Why

Campaign Analytics is a 2-page marketing intelligence dashboard built in Power BI to evaluate the performance and profitability of ad campaigns across Google Ads, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Email, and Unknown channels.

The goal was to go beyond surface-level metrics — telling a data story that helps marketing stakeholders make decisions around budget reallocation, channel prioritisation, targeting improvement, and ROI maximisation.

Each page answers a distinct business question, with an insight panel surfacing the "so what" directly on the dashboard.

Business Objectives

  • Identify which channels generate the highest ROI and profit
  • Compare spend vs revenue across all marketing channels
  • Analyse CTR, CPC, and conversion rate per channel
  • Surface underperforming channels draining budget without returns

Key Insights

What the Data Said

🏆

Google Ads Leads ROI

Google Ads delivers an ROI of 7.03 with a low CPC of ₹1.29 — the best performing channel by far. Budget should be prioritised here for maximum return per rupee spent.

📧

Email is the Most Expensive Channel

Email has the highest CPC at ₹3.89 and one of the lowest ROIs at 2.01 — indicating a high cost-per-result relative to other channels. Needs optimisation or reduced spend.

📊

CTR & Conversion Are Nearly Identical

CTR (~3.01%) and Conversion (~11.17%) are almost uniform across all channels, suggesting poor targeting differentiation — a signal that audience segmentation strategy needs revisiting.

📸

Instagram is a Strong #2

Instagram delivers an ROI of 4.50 with a low CPC of ₹1.23 — the second best performer. It combines strong returns with efficient spend, making it a reliable growth channel.

❓

Unknown Channel Needs Tracking Fix

The "Unknown" channel shows a surprisingly high CTR of 3.25% but an ROI of only 2.03 — indicating untracked traffic that may be misattributed. Tracking validation is critical before scaling.

📉

Facebook Has Lowest ROI

Facebook ranks last with an ROI of 1.77 and a high CPC of ₹2.84. Despite decent CTR and conversion rates, the cost structure makes it the least efficient channel in the mix.

What I Learned

Key Takeaways

ROI Alone Doesn't Tell the Full Story

A channel with high ROI but low volume may not scale. Looking at ROI alongside total profit and spend together gives a more complete picture of channel value.

Uniform Metrics Signal a Strategy Problem

When CTR and conversion rates are nearly identical across all channels, it's not a data issue — it's a targeting problem. Analytics revealed a strategic gap, not just a number.

CPC Is a Hidden Budget Killer

Email's high CPC (₹3.89 vs Google Ads' ₹1.29) quietly drains budget. Tracking cost efficiency per click alongside ROI exposed where money was being wasted.

Data Quality Gates Decisions

The "Unknown" channel had strong CTR but no reliable attribution — proving that decisions made on unvalidated data can be misleading. Tracking hygiene is a prerequisite for insight.
